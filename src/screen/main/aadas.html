Storage of Blood Product
1. Blood bank is the only place where blood is stored (not in ward or domestic fridge).
 2. Blood should be collected from blood bank only when it is required, and patient is ready for transfusion.
 3. Unused blood and blood component should be returned to blood bank within 30 minutes. (30 minutes rule)
4. Platelets is not stored in Blood bank Ahmadi Hospital; it is ordered from Kuwaiti Central Blood bank for definite patient.
5. Pediatric blood is less than 5 days; therefore, it is not stored in Blood bank fridge, ordered from KCBB corresponding to both                 baby & mother blood type & volume requested for transfusion

Indication for PRBCs
  Clinical signs and symptoms of severe anemia.
  Evidence of acute loss of 15% of total blood volume (>750 ml).
     Hemoglobin of 7 g/dl or less. 
    Hemoglobin of 8 g/dl or less for patients undergoing orthopedic or cardiac surgery.
    Other indication such as thalassemia major patient) 
Indication for Platelets concentration  
    Actively bleeding patient:
       To keep platelet counts above 50x109 /L & above 100x109 if there is disseminated intravascular coagulation.
       For bleeding due to platelet dysfunction.
       Patients having cardiopulmonary by-pass (CPM) who exhibit perioperative bleeding and /or platelet dysfunction.
  Preparation of invasive procedure 
          Elective central venous catheter placement with platelet count <20x109 /L.
        Elective diagnostic lumbar puncture with platelet count <50x109/L.
        Neuro or ocular surgery with platelet count <100x109/L.
        Major surgery or delivery with platelet count <50x109/L.
        Neuroaxial anesthesia with platelet count <80x189/L. 
          Percutaneous liver biopsy with platelet count <50x109/L 
     Prevention of bleeding
       Bone marrow suppression with platelet count <10x109/L. 

   Indication for Fresh Frozen Plasma FFP
        Major bleeding or preparation for an urgent invasive procedure.
        Treatment of multiple coagulation factor deficiencies.
        Massive transfusion protocol. 
           Plasma exchange for thrombotic thrombocytopenia purpura.
   Indication for Cryoprecipitate 
          Plasma fibrinogen level below 100 mg/dl or dysfunctional fibrinogen. 
          FXIII deficiency.

Product selection

     Rhesus factor (RH) compatibility
        Only applied to packed red blood cell (PRBC) and platelet concentration.
        Rh positive patient can receive Rh + or Rh- component. 
          Rh negative patient preferably receives Rh- component except in urgent cases if Rhcomponent not available and patient either male or             female above age of 50.
      PRBC 
           Blood Group (ABO) compatibility is the first choice.
         If ABO compatibility is not available, it shifts to the second and third choice consecutively. 
         Platelet Concentration 
             ABO compatibility is the first choice, but any ABO type component may be used. 
              In case of platelets refractoriness, group specific and Human Leukocyte Antigen (HLA) matched maybe indicated.
        Cryoprecipitate
         ABO compatibility is the first choice, but any ABO type component may be used. 
         FFP 
            Blood Group (ABO) compatibility is the first choice.
          If ABO compatibility is not available, it shifts to the second and third choice consecutively. 
Rh Compatibility 
            Rh (+ve) Positive Patient
                      Can receive either Rh+ or Rh- components
         Rh (-ve) Negative Patient
                       Preferably receive Rh- components
                       Rh(+ve) may be given for urgent cases if Rh(-ve) is not available and patient is either male or “female above age of 50”

Dosage

PRBCs  
     250 ml PRBC unit raises (Hb) of an average size adult by 1 g/dl, HCT 3-4 %
     Non-Bleeding cases
       Transfused blood unit-by-unit basis according to patient symptoms In Chronic anemia & Cardiac disease, rate of administration does not          exceed 20- 40 drops/min
     Actively bleeding cases
       Supportive blood transfusion until control bleeding
       Activate massive transfusions Protocol if:
                1. Actual or anticipated blood transfusion of 4 units PRBC in 4 hours
                2. Hemodynamically unstable patient
                3. Anticipated ongoing bleeding 
Platelet Concentration
         6-unit pooled platelet concentrate/ 1 unit apheresis platelet should increase the platelet count approximately 25-50 x109 /L
         Reassess the patient’s clinical condition and check platelets count, after each transfusion
      Give further doses if needed. 
        Single dose of platelets for Routine Transfusion 
        More than single dose of platelets Severe thrombocytopenia, bleeding in critical
       site:
           1. Central Nervous System
           2. eyes 
       Insufficient platelets increment causes
            1. Increase consumption (sever sepsis), DIC
            2. Platelet destruction (sever sepsis, drug induced)
            3. Platelet refractoriness:
            4. Allo-immunization 

Fresh Frozen Plasma FFP
           Each FFP unit volume 200 ml, dose of 10-15 ml/Kg of Patient body weight  
Cryoprecipitate  
             Dose of 1 unit / 10 Kg of patient body weight  

Infusion Rate 

PRBCs 
            1. First 15 minutes
               1-2 ml/min (60-120 ml/hour)
            2. After 15 minutes
                As rapidly as tolerated; approx. 4 ml/min (240 ml/hour)
            3. Pediatric Dosage/rate
                10 ml/Kg; not to exceed 15 ml/Kg at 2-5 ml/Kg/hour
            4. Special Consideration
                 1-2 hours for hemodynamically stable recipient; infusion should not exceed 4 hrs. adjust flow rate to 1ml/Kg/hour for patient at risk                   of fluid overload. 

Platelet Concentration
             1. First 15 minutes
                 2-5 ml/min (120–300 ml/hour)
              2. After 15 minutes
                 As rapidly as tolerated; approx. 300 ml/hour
              3. Pediatric Dosage/rate
                  5-10 ml/Kg; not to exceed 15 ml/Kg at 2-5 ml/Kg/hour
              4. special Consideration
                Usually given over 1-2 hours For patients at risk of fluid overload, use slower flow rate 

Fresh Frozen Plasma FFP
               1. First 15 minutes
                   2-5 ml/min (120-300 ml/hour)
               2. After 15 minutes
                    As rapidly as tolerated; approx. 300 ml/hour
               3. Pediatric Dosage/rate
                   Acute hemorrhage: 15-30 ml/Kg as indicated
                    Clotting deficiency: 10-15 ml/kg at 1-2 ml/min
                4. Special Consideration
                     Thaw time needed before issue.
                     Expiration- 24 hours after thaw time
                    Do Not hang longer than 24 hours
                    For patient at risk of fluid overload, use slower flow rate 

Cryoprecipitate
                   1. First 15 minutes
                       As rapidly as tolerated
                   2. After 15 minutes
                       As rapidly as tolerated; approx. 5-10 ml/min
                   3. Pediatric Dosage/rate
                       1-2 U/10 Kg, not faster than 1 ml/Kg/min
                   4. Special Consideration
                         Infuse ASAP after thawing; pooling is preferred
                         Maximum benefit if used within 2 hours

Special Request 

Irradiated Blood
Used for PRBC and platelet Concentration to prevent (TA_GVHD) 
Indication for: 
 First & second-degree relative donors
 Intrauterine transfusion
 Neonatal transfusion/exchange transfusion 
   Patient on purine analogues, anti-thymocyte or alemtuzumab 
   Hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT)

Washed Blood 
Used for PRBC and platelet Concentration to eliminate plasma from the blood product 
Indication for: 
  Severe or recurrent allergic reaction
  History of anaphylaxis 
    Immunoglobulin A (IgA) deficiency
 Patient at risk for hypokalemia
 Transfusion of the washed unit should be completed within 24 hrs. for PRBC & within 4 hrs. for platelet concentration 

Sickle Cell Negative RBC
Indication for: 
  Intrauterine transfusion
  Neonate 
    Patient with sickle cell disease.
